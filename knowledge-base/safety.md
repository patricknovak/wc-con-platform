## Operational Safety Standards

### Site Safety Protocols
- **Pre-Delivery Assessment**: Sites evaluated for safe access and unloading
- **Traffic Control**: Professional traffic management for hazardous operations
- **Hazard Identification**: Systematic process identifies and mitigates site hazards
- **Worker Protection**: PPE requirements enforced; hard hats, safety vests, steel-toe boots mandatory
- **Communication**: Clear signage, spotters for blind spots, radio communication

### Equipment Safety
- **Regular Maintenance**: All equipment serviced per manufacturer specifications
- **Pre-Operation Checks**: Daily safety inspections before use
- **ROPS/FOPS**: Rollover and falling object protection systems on all mobile equipment
- **Visibility**: Backup cameras, mirrors, warning lights on all vehicles
- **Emergency Equipment**: First aid kits, spill kits, fire extinguishers on all trucks

### Driver & Operator Qualifications
- **Class 3 Licenses**: All truck drivers hold valid commercial Class 3 licenses
- **Air Brake Certification**: Required for all dump truck and pup trailer operators
- **Heavy Equipment Certification**: Excavator, loader, dozer operators fully certified
- **Medical Assessment**: Drivers pass medical exam annually
- **Defensive Driving**: All drivers complete defensive driving training
- **Continued Training**: Annual refresher courses on new regulations and safety practices

### Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
- **Mandatory Items**: Hard hat, safety vest, safety glasses, work gloves, steel-toe boots
- **Site-Specific**: Additional PPE required based on site assessment (respirator, hearing protection, etc.)
- **Supply**: WC-CON provides or reimburses reasonable PPE costs
- **Enforcement**: No-work policy without proper PPE
- **Maintenance**: All PPE inspected and replaced as needed

## Incident Management & Reporting

### Incident Response Protocol
1. **Immediate Response**: Ensure safety of all personnel; provide first aid if needed
2. **Scene Security**: Establish safe perimeter; prevent further incidents
3. **Notification**: Report to supervisor, safety officer, and emergency services if needed
4. **Documentation**: Photograph scene; collect witness statements; record all details
5. **Investigation**: Root cause analysis conducted within 24 hours
6. **Corrective Action**: Implement measures to prevent recurrence
7. **Communication**: Notify relevant parties (WCB, client, regulator if applicable)

### Reporting Timeline
- **Immediate** (0-1 hour): Internal notification and first aid
- **24 Hours**: WCB incident reporting if required
- **48 Hours**: Incident investigation completed
- **1 Week**: Corrective action plan finalized
- **Ongoing**: Follow-up audits to verify corrective actions

### Records Management
- **Retention**: All safety records retained for minimum 7 years
- **Accessibility**: Records available for audit and inspection
- **Privacy**: Personal medical information handled confidentially
- **Continuous Improvement**: Data analyzed to identify trends and patterns

## Hazard-Specific Safety Information

### Aggregate Loading & Transportation
- **Hazards**: Flying debris, load shifting, equipment contact
- **Controls**: Load securement, tarping when required, spotters for backing
- **PPE**: Hard hat, safety vest, eye protection mandatory
- **Training**: All personnel trained on load securement regulations

### Equipment Operation
- **Hazards**: Operator contact, bystander contact, tip-over
- **Controls**: ROPS, visibility enhancements, traffic control, worker training
- **Maintenance**: Daily pre-op checks, scheduled servicing
- **Certification**: Only qualified operators permit

### Dust Control
- **Hazard**: Inhalation of dust particles
- **Control**: Water trucks for dust suppression at loading/unloading
- **Equipment**: High-efficiency dustless loaders used when feasible
- **PPE**: Respirators provided when needed

### Chemical Safety
- **Fuel/Hydraulics**: Secondary containment, spill kits on all trucks
- **Hazmat**: Proper handling, labeling, disposal of hazardous materials
- **Training**: All staff trained on chemical hazards relevant to their role

## Reporting & Escalation

### Safety Concerns
Contact immediately:
- **Site Supervisor**: For on-site hazards or concerns
- **Safety Officer**: For general safety issues or violations
- **Management**: (780) 865-6000 for serious concerns
- **WCB**: For workers to report safety violations to regulator

### Emergency Response
- **911**: For life-threatening emergencies
- **Dispatch**: (780) 865-6000 for equipment or transportation emergencies
- **Safety Officer**: For workplace incidents requiring investigation

### Anonymous Reporting
- **Hazard Hotline**: Available for anonymous reporting of safety concerns
- **Confidentiality**: Reporter identity protected
- **Investigation**: All reports investigated and acted upon
